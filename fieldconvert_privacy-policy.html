1. Overview

FieldConvert is designed around a simple job: helping users convert values between measurement systems such as metric, US, and imperial. Because the product is utility-focused, the privacy goal is also straightforward: keep data handling limited, practical, and proportionate to the features being offered.

The product is intended to support core conversion behavior with minimal dependence on continuous connectivity. That means core use may rely primarily on local resources rather than constant network activity.

2. Information FieldConvert may handle

Depending on the features you use, FieldConvert may handle the following categories of information:

  • conversion inputs you enter into the product
  • recent conversion history saved on your device
  • favorites and saved presets you create
  • product preferences, such as selected unit-system defaults and language settings
  • basic technical information required for the product to function reliably on your device

FieldConvert is not presented as a social platform, messaging service, or behavioral advertising product. The product direction is practical utility, not broad personal profiling.

3. How information is used

Information handled by FieldConvert may be used to:

  • perform conversions you request
  • remember your preferred unit system, such as Metric, US, or Imperial
  • show your recent history, saved favorites, and saved presets
  • support language availability where product resources exist
  • maintain reliability, troubleshoot issues, and improve the product over time

The product intent is functional convenience. Data use should align with utility features rather than unrelated marketing behavior.

4. Offline-ready storage

FieldConvert is designed so key utility flows can remain useful without requiring continuous connectivity. As part of that design, some information such as history, favorites, presets, and settings may be stored locally on your device.

Local storage allows the product to reopen in a familiar state and helps repeat tasks take fewer steps. Where data is stored locally for product functionality, it is generally used to support your direct use of FieldConvert rather than to build a broader profile about you.

5. Data sharing and third parties

Based on the current FieldConvert product direction reflected in this repository, FieldConvert is not presented as a data-broker, social-sharing, or ad-targeting product.

If third-party services are introduced in the future for analytics, crash reporting, support workflows, hosting, or infrastructure, this policy should be updated to describe those services and the relevant data categories more specifically.

6. Retention and deletion

Information that exists only in local product storage may remain on your device until you remove it, clear product data, or delete the product from your device.

If future versions of FieldConvert add cloud-backed features or support channels that collect submitted information, retention periods should be documented more explicitly at that time.

7. Security

Reasonable efforts should be taken to protect information handled by FieldConvert. However, no software environment can promise absolute security. You should avoid using a general utility product like FieldConvert to store highly sensitive personal, financial, medical, or regulated information unless the product is clearly redesigned and documented for that purpose.

8. Children’s privacy

FieldConvert is a general-purpose utility product and is not specifically directed to children. The current product direction does not rely on child-oriented engagement patterns or sensitive-profile features.

9. International use and language

Public-facing FieldConvert documentation is maintained in English for consistency. If additional language support is introduced, this policy may be updated to reflect those changes more specifically.

10. Policy changes

This privacy policy may be updated as FieldConvert evolves. If the product adds materially different data practices, networked services, or support workflows, the policy should be revised to reflect those changes more specifically.
